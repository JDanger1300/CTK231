Background

I have been playing guitar for over half of my life, and bass for nearly as long. I have lent my skills to a considerable number of bands in the Chicago area. In addition to my personal music endeavors, I participated in the music programs in both middle and high school. I was in choir for 7 years, which enabled me to travel to New York and perform at Carnegie Hall in an honor choir on two sepaerate occasions. I was part of the vocal jazz small group at my high school as a baritone/bass, as well as the all men's acapella group. I also participated in my school's jazz and pep bands as both a guitarist and bassist, and took home the Louis Armstrong Jazz Award for outstanding performance in my senior year of high school.

Performing

The vast majority of my participation in music comes from performing live. I started playing at a local coffee shop in my hometown when I was 12, and played there every weekend for 3 years up until 2020. I then started a band with some of my high school friends and we played local bars and festivals up through graduation, which is when I left. The band is still going strong today with new members. I spent the next couple years filling in for whoever needed me, house bands at open mics and jams, acoustic duos and trios, even Cadillac Grove, a 10 piece rock band that plays all over the area. Currently, I am the lead guitarist in a duo with the very talented Jack Wiemeler, we average about 1 gig per week. In addition to our duo shows, I am the bassist for our full band and we are working on our first album, all original hard rock and grunge.

Other Music Projects

Besides performing live, I am working on several other music projects, some for school and some for fun. I am currently in the process of writing and creating original music to serve as the soundtrack for RetroShift Digital's final installment of Space Altercations in Space. I am also working on a parody of Mountain's "Theme to an Imaginary Western" for the end credits. Besides that, I have a Bandcamp page for some of my school projects, including some drum loops, foley narratives, and a heavy interpolation of Beck's "Earthquake Weather".
